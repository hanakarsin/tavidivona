    KBC staff down tools again following six months without pay

    - The 70 correspondents said they would not provide content until they are paid their dues - The employees who downed tool work for both KBC TV and Coro FM where they provide about 70% of news items - This is the second time the correspondents are going on strike due to lack of pay

    21 Jul 2024, 00:00

    - The 70 correspondents said they would not provide content until they are paid their dues

    - The employees who downed tool work for both KBC TV and Coro FM where they provide about 70% of news items

    - This is the second time the correspondents are going on strike due to lack of pay

    About 70 correspondents working for KBC television and Coro FM have downed their tools after going for six months without receiving their salaries.

    The journalists, 50 in the TV section and 16 supplying content to Coro FM cited going for six months without pay despite working around the clock to supply the national broadcaster with news from all over the country.

    “It is very sad to see all other artists getting paid while we supply the national broadcaster with 75% of what it airs," said a TV correspondent who declined to be identified for fear of victimisation.

    This is not the first time the correspondents are going on strike over pay issue.

    It is only four months since the said employees walked to former Information Principal Secretary Sammy Itemere to air their grievances when KBC management succumbed to pressure from higher offices and paid them only two months dues.

    The correspondents went to see the PS after working for seven months without pay.

    “Does it mean that the Finance Manager KBC will only be acting on our case after pressure from higher authorities? Are we not entitled to payments of our hard earned money?

    "We buy cameras, recorders and mics on our own and KBC does not supply us with any working material. So it’s only noble for the station to pay us so that we stop being evicted from our houses and running away from those we owe,” said another bitter correspondent.

    They added they foresee a situation where by they will go for December holidays without pay as it has always been the case.

    “This has been the trend, every time we go for holidays without pay and when other children are treated by their parents, we avoid shame by taking ours to rural areas to their grandparents for lack of money,” another KBC employee told TUKO.co.ke on Thursday, October 18.

    The correspondents vowed not to get back to work until they receive at least four months salary adding it will be exactly seven months of no pay by the end of October 2018.

    Two of the correspondents complained of family members who have been sick but have been forced to remain in hospitals as the employees do not have the means of paying the bills.

    “We are not asking much. Just pay what is rightfully ours," the correspondents said.

    The Parliamentary committee on ICT had earlier summoned top officials of the national broadcaster to shed more light on the financial crisis surrounding the media house.

    The broadcaster has been entangled in unending debts even after not submitting deductions of it’s employees.

    The workers have also been denied services by respective Saccos since the corporation does not submit their remittances.
