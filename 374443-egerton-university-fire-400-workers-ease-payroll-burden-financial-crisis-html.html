    Egerton University to fire over 400 workers to ease payroll burden amid financial crisis

    - Egerton University said it was looking to ease its payroll by KSh 38.7 monthly by sacking the employees - In a report, the institution said government's funding had dipped since 2015 - The university said students who were out of school since March were the main source of funding for the school

    12 Apr 2024, 00:00

    - Egerton University said it was looking to ease its payroll by KSh 38.7 monthly by sacking the employees

    - In a report, the institution said government's funding had dipped since 2015

    - The university said students who were out of school since March were the main source of funding for the school

    Egerton University has conveyed plans to sack 400 staffers citing cash crisis worsened by the COVID-19 pandemic.

    The institution proposed retrenching the employees to reduce the payroll cost by KSh 38.7 million per month, a decision which, the university council had approved.

    In a report, the university said the government funding had plummeted since 2015 further worsening the financial situation of the cash-strapped institution.

    It, therefore, had no source of funding except its students who have been out of school since March 2020 when the government closed down learning institutions due to COVID-19.

    “This will reduce the payroll cost by Sh38.7 million per month,” read the report.

    The report indicated the learners pay KSh 16,000 as school fees annually.

    In a separate story, Education Cabinet Secretary George Magoha ruled out the reopening of universities and colleges in September 2020 as earlier indicated.

    The CS noted most of the institutions were still unprepared and would not have met the set protocols by September as envisioned by the ministry.
