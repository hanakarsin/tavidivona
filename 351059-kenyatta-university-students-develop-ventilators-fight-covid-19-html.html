    Kenyatta University students develop ventilators in fight against COVID-19

    - A group of 16 students from different faculties worked together to develop the ventilator's prototype - The students were from the school of Biomedical Engineering, Electrical Engineering, Pharmacy, Medicine, and Nursing

    05 Aug 2024, 00:00

    - A group of 16 students from different faculties worked together to develop the ventilator's prototype

    - The students were from the school of Biomedical Engineering, Electrical Engineering, Pharmacy, Medicine, and Nursing

    - The students who worked under the guidance of their lecture said they could manufacture 50 ventilators in a week

    - The prototypes were awaiting certification and approval from the government

    - The students expressed optimism that the certification would be done by Monday, April 13, 2020

    A group of 16 engineering students from Kenyatta University (KU) have developed the much needed respirators in the fight against the COVID-19 pandemic.

    The students from different faculties embarked on the venture to develop the ventilator's prototypes within a week putting in 18 hours a day.

    Unveiling the apparatus on Saturday, April 11, the students through the institution's innovation and incubation centre said they could manufacture 50 ventilators in a week.

    "The urge to make this machine is due to the shortage currently being experienced in the country," one of the students said. "We all know that other countries have locked their borders and cannot export these ventilators, yet we direly need them," he added.

    READ ALSO: Six Kenyan clinical officers test positive for COVID-19

    According to the students, the prototypes were awaiting certification and approval from the government while expressing optimism that it would be done by Monday, April 13, 2020.

    If all goes well, they will begin the mass production of the ventilators to help the country's hospitals to carter for patients of the COVID-19 pandemic.

    "We have been working with a team of 16 students from the school of Biomedical Engineering, Electrical Engineering, Pharmacy, Medicine, and Nursing to make these ventilators," the student said.

    The stand showcasing the machines was visited by the Trade and Industrialization CS Betty Maina, accompanied by the Permanent Secretary (PS) Industrialization.

    Kenyatta University Vice-Chancellor Paul Wainaina was also present to witness the innovation.

    When university students joined the rest of the learners from secondary and primary schools in going home following a government's directive, Kenyans online taunted them.

    They argued that their peers in other countries were taking up roles in helping in research to fight the pandemic and that they should have done the same instead of going home.

    The new development has been received with positively by the same Kenyans who had taunted the students and are now applauding the 16 geniuses.

    "Incredible Kenyatta University, this is the way to go. I want to see the University of Nairobi Chiromo campus working on a vaccine," tweeted Charlie Oddie "Moi University in Rivatex manufacturing gloves and masks, Egerton University producing food for relief and many other innovations across universities," he added.
