    Kalonzo Musyoka Asks Raila Odinga to Join OKA, Be His 2022 Presidential Running Mate

    Wiper leader Kalonzo Musyoka has asked former prime minister Raila Odinga to join the One Kenya Alliance (OKA) and be his running mate in the 2022 presidential poll.

    Speaking in Changamwe, Mombasa, on Friday, July 30, Kalonzo argued he had supported the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) leader for several years, and it was his time to reciprocate.

    "I welcome my big brother Odinga who I have supported immensely in his political career for more than a decade. What I am saying is that there is no political debt, but I want him to use his wisdom and join me as my running mate and move this country forward," Kalonzo said.

    The former vice president insisted he won't be a peacemaker anymore and would go all the way to the wire.

    "Let's be focused this time, and I am going to be a presidential candidate in the 2022 General Election. I do not want to be a peacemaker anymore. I will no longer be considered a weak candidate this time for the sake of peace," he maintained as quoted by Daily Nation.

    ODM ditches NASA

    Kalonzo spoke a day after ODM in a statement said it was quitting the NASA coalition to focus on building new friends and partners as it prepares to contest in next year's polls.

    The party's governing council resolved to have the opposition party formally exit the coalition to fully focus on the future as it embarks on building new partnerships.

    "We still hold that NASA is the past. To further demonstrate this point, the National Executive Committee (NEC) of ODM has today resolved to formally exit the NASA coalition," said a statement read by the party's secretary-general Edwin Sifuna.

    Kalonzo says he won't back Raila for third time

    Kalonzo previously stated he would not support Raila again for the presidency after their previous plans failed to materialise.

    He stated Raila had never sought him to discuss plans ahead of the 2022 elections.

    He further insisted that it would be a tall order for him to support the ODM leader again, given the two had worked together previously, with the ODM chief failing to reciprocate the support.

    "I am not a weakling...Raila and I have never sat to discuss 2022. I will be the most stupid person on earth to support Raila Odinga for president for the third time without a measure of reciprocity," said Kalonzo.
