    What should you consider when choosing an internet service provider?

    Here are the most important factors to keep in mind.

    • Internet speed and signal strength
    • Internet prices and cost
    • Router portability (for those who like to be on the move)
    • Redundancy

    How much is Wi-Fi per month in Kenya?

    The prices vary among the different service providers. Typically, Wi-Fi price in Kenya ranges from about Ksh 1,500 to about Ksh 12,000.

    What is a WiFi router price in Kenya on Jumia?

    WiFi router prices in Nairobi, particularly on Jumia, can vary depending on the type. A TP-Link 300mbps wireless WiFi router goes for Ksh 2,479 while the Tenda N300 300 Mbps wireless WiFi router goes for Ksh 1,947.

    How much does it cost to install Wi-Fi at home in Kenya?

    Fibre-to-the-home installations are typically free. For wireless connections, customers often have to pay an initial installation fee for the routers. The Wi-Fi installation cost in Kenya varies from one service provider to the other.

    Which is the best Wi-Fi for a home in Kenya?

    Telkom and Airtel have some of the cheapest Wi-Fi packages in Kenya. These ISPs have numerous daily, weekly, and monthly Wi-Fi packages at very affordable rates.

    What is the cheapest way to get Wi-Fi at home?

    The least expensive option involves going for providers with fibre internet connections since they typically do not charge installation fees.

    You have the choice to acquire Safaricom's 4G+ internet, starting at Ksh 2,999 for 140 GB in the cheapest package. Alternatively, you can select the Fibre-To-The-Home option, with the bronze package offering 10 Mbps at the same price of Ksh 2,999.

    How much is MiFi per month in Kenya?

    The prices for universal MiFi routers that are compatible with Safaricom, Airtel, and Telkom range from Ksh 4,000, the cheapest, to Ksh 18,000, the most expensive.
