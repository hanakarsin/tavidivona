    Kenyans Come to Rescue of Single Mother Who Was Sleeping on Floor after Leaving Toxic Marriage

    Kenyans have come to the rescue of a single mother of one child who has been sleeping on the floor after leaving a toxic marriage early this year.

    Winfred Mwende posted a picture of her humble house and beddings on the popular Facebook group Glam My Home and interestingly, she was not asking for help but the tide turned to her favour as many were touched by her post.

    Speaking to TUKO.co.ke, the mother of one who is also expecting her second born in a few months' time said when she made that post, she had seen other posts of people sleeping on the floor and so she did not think hers would be any different or would receive any attention.

    However, many generous Kenyans started reaching out especially offering her goodies like beds, mattresses, duvets and other household items.

    The woman said besides the household items, she also received financial donations, job offers which she will hopefully get after she gives birth, and other goodies.

    "At least now we can sleep on a bed which is much warmer than on the floor. Also, I am yet to receive a tabletop cooker from Cool Star Refrigerators Malindi; it's on the way. Now I have hopes to set up a small salon from the few who sent me M-Pesa. There's progress from where I was," she told TUKO.co.ke.

    The best part is that she sees a future for her baby and the one she will soon welcome into the world.

    Leaving a toxic marriage

    The strong woman said she took a step of faith in January 2021 to leave a toxic union that had pushed her to the brink of depression and suicide as she had attempted to take her own life last year.

    She said she decided to leave when she realised she was expecting her secondborn albeit she literally had nothing.

    "I realised if I stayed I might slip back into depression since he was an emotionally unavailable person and I might not come out alive. However, leaving had its fair share of backlash and ‘shame' but I said to myself that they're all background noises that are meant to distract me. I was after peace even if it meant sleeping hungry," she said.

    She is now looking into the future with a glimmer of hope and though she is yet to get some of the items promised by well-wishers, she is sure her life will get better.

    In a separate story, TUKO.co.ke also brought you the heartwarming story of Pauline Muiruri who was trolled for having simple wall decors.

    However, in the midst of this dark cloud, a silver lining emerged after one photographer, Jackson Mwangi, posted in the group that he was ready to offer free shoot for the woman and her family and also give her free wall mounts so that she could decorate her walls.

    After the photographer's post, Sharh On a Mission also said she will give the woman a cake to celebrate her new home-makeover.

    One by one, well-wishers and companies promising her shopping vouchers, luxurious doormats for her home, new African themed clothes, wall clocks, more lunch offers in huge hotels trickled in and what started out as an embarrassing situation left her basking in blessings.
