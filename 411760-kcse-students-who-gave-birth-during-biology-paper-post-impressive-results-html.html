    Two girls who sat for the 2020 Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) examinations have been celebrated for posting impressive results despite giving birth in the middle of their tests.

    The two students from a Kisii-based secondary school scored a B- in their examinations to the pleasant surprise of their teachers and parents.

    According to a report by The Standard, Charity, one of the girls gave birth to a beautiful baby girl on the day of her Biology and Kiswahili papers.

    She scored a B in both subjects.

    "It was a struggle but I thank God I made it through. I will take my time before I decide which course I will pursue," the young mother said.

    Her parents said their daughter's performance had made them incredibly proud especially given the circumstances under which she sat for the papers.

    "It was a challenge for me and the entire family (when they learned of her pregnancy) but I am glad she has made it through and she is now qualified to join the university," her father said.

    The other female student gave birth on the same day she completed her national tests and also scored a B-.

    According to the school principal, four candidates in the school were expectant by the time of sitting for their KCSE.

    They were under a special diet in order to keep them nourished as they prepared for their national examinations.

    652 pregnant girls

    Speaking on Monday, May 10, at Mitihani House while releasing the results, Education CS George Magoha said Bungoma county had the highest number of female candidates (43) who gave birth while sitting for exams.

    Bungoma was followed by Meru which had 38 candidates, Nakuru (36), Kisii (36) and Nandi, 31.

    Generally, of the 366,834 female candidates who sat for the national exam, the tough-talking CS said 652 of them wrote their papers in hospitals after childbirth.
