    Mike Sonko has dismissed President Uhuru Kenyatta's remarks that he made him ascend to the plum seat of Nairobi governorship in the 2017 polls.

    The head of state in an interview with a vernacular station revealed that he made Sonko the governor and initiated his impeachment over nonperformance.

    However, while responding to the president's remarks, the flamboyant politician in a statement on Monday, January 18, dismissed the claims insisting governors are not presidential appointees and that he was validly voted in by around one million Nairobians.

    "For the record, it is not Uhuru Kenyatta who made me the Governor of Nairobi. I was validly elected by close to one million residents of Nairobi, garnering more votes than the President himself, and Governors are not Presidential appointees," said Sonko.

    The ex- Nairobi boss first called out Uhuru for addressing a section of Kenyans in his vernacular saying it was a gross violation of Article 131 (1) (e) of the Constitution of Kenya

    Development in Nairobi

    The former Makadara lawmaker went ahead to dismiss Uhuru's claims that he was impeached because of being unable to deliver to the Nairobians.

    He insisted that his development track record speaks for itself not limited to timely garbage collection, roads and fire stations.

    Sonko said his administration was doing so well before being disrupted by the head of state and his family over what he termed as a "political witch hunt" that culminated in his dismissal.

    He said his administration was performing better than the national government and so, it was ridiculous for the Jubilee party leader to label him a nonperformer.

    Uhuru's failed administration

    Sonko in the statement turned tables on the head of state over performance claiming he has presided over the worst administration in the history of Kenya.

    He said President Uhuru Kenyatta had nothing to show despite borrowing over KSh 8 trillion in his eight years of administration.

    "Despite borrowing over 8 trillion shillings in the last 8 years, what does the President have to show apart from unprecedented unemployment, poverty and hopelessness in millions of Kenyans?" said Sonko.

    He added that millions of jobs he promised to the youth remained a mirage and he is now resorting to selling false hope in the name of Handshake and BBI.

    He reiterated ex-Chief Justice David Maraga statement that he should have been impeached by now.
