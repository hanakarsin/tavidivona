    Boni Khalwale proposes Philomena Mwilu as Kenya's first female chief justice: &quot;Confirm her&quot;

    Former Kakamega senator Boni Khalwale has proposed the appointment of Deputy Chief Justice Philomena Mwilu as the first female chief justice.

    While admitting that having women in top leadership positions in Kenya remains a pipe dream, the politician said Mwilu, who is already serving as the chief justice albeit in an acting capacity, should be given the chance to lead the judiciary.

    Taking to his Twitter on Tuesday, January 12, Khalwale said her confirmation should be made without further ado as her time has come.

    "A woman is not going to be President in Kenya any time soon. None has ever served as Speaker of Parliament. Ascending to the helm in the Judiciary is a tall order and now Justice Philomena Mwilu has emerged. Confirm her as our new Chief Justice," he wrote.

    However, some Kenyans did not concur with him and even accused him of playing the gender card in the appointment of chief justice while others supported his sentiments.

    Akinyi Abong'o said:

    "Why are you pulling the gender narrative if she is qualified why not?"

    Abra Hams wrote:

    "You are destroying her slim chance. But it's intentional".

    Bahati Okunu said:

    "Leadership isn't about gender or tribe, it's about the capability of that person. Stop bringing sexism here".

    Nick said:

    "I agree with you! I wish they could just let the natural justice flow".

    As reported by TUKO.co.ke earlier, Maraga passed the baton to Mwilu in a ceremony convened at the Supreme Court on Monday, January 11, ahead of his retirement which is set to kick off today, Tuesday, January 12, when he turns 70.

    Activist Okiya Omtatah had earlier moved to court to block Mwilu from taking over as the next President of the Supreme Court.
