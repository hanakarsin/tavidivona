    Kericho Governor Paul Chepkwony has said he lives a modest life in an old house that was built in 1937, and in which he only has two workers; a gardener and a cook.

    The second-time county boss said he does not believe in an over the top lifestyle and that is why he only has one security officer.

    Speaking to the Kenyan Historian YouTube channel, the politician said sometimes, having too many bodyguards attracts enemies and always indicates that someone is hiding something.

    So simple is Chepkwony's life that he does not use a fleet of cars while making his travels as opposed to his counterparts who travel with several escort cars.

    "It only take me one car to travel to Nairobi so I do not need many cars, one is enough," he said.

    The county chief said he lives in a simple house that was built in 1937, saying he sleeps well and is often peaceful in his residence.

    "This house was built in 1937, a very old house. The floor is actually made of timber. No matter how big a residence is, you can only sleep in one bedroom, in a tiny bed in a corner. All I need is what is adequate for me," he said.

    Chepkwony meets nurse

    TUKO.co.ke also reported when Chepkwony met a nurse from Kericho who warmed the hearts of many Kenyans after helping a patient with her baby.

    Nurse Velma Tonui from Londiani sub-county hospital in Kericho was going about her day in the facility when a woman who was in labour was brought to the hospital.

    Tonui recalled that the woman's husband had to rush to take care of the registration process and so the woman's baby boy was left without a caregiver as the mother was in labour.

    Her first instinct was to help the mother take care of the little boy and so she carried the boy on her back and still continued taking care of the woman who was set to deliver any moment.
