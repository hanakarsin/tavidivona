    Escaping Persecution? Tanzanian Opposition Politicians Flee to Kenya over Safety Concerns

    - Four Tanzanian Opposition politicians have fled to Kenya citing safety concerns in their home country - The politicians who hail from Rorya district in the north of the country, were council ward representatives between 2015 and 2020

    Four Tanzanian Opposition politicians have fled to Kenya citing safety concerns even as President John Pombe Magufuli's whereabouts remain unknown for 18 days now.

    Magufuli has not appeared in public for a couple of weeks with his office remaining tight-lipped about his whereabouts even as speculation about his health continues.

    The politicians, who hail from Rorya district in the north of the country, held council ward representative positions between 2015 and 2020.

    They were elected on Chadema party tickets.

    The four claimed they are facing persecution for rejecting the October 2020 poll results which saw Magufuli re-elected as the president.

    Ngai Kagose Mawinda of Rabour Ward and his counterparts, Lazaro Edwin of Mkoma Ward, Edward Ndege of Nyaongo Ward and Andrew Nyakriga of Ikoma Ward are currently in Nairobi after crossing the border on Monday, March 15 and Tuesday, March 17.

    According to the lawyer of the four politicians, George Wajackoyah, the representatives were seeking refuge in Kenya due to threats they were facing in their home country.

    “I have been arrested and taken to court on trumped-up charges yet I was just complaining against ballot stuffing. I fear for my life and cannot bear living in Tanzania anymore,” said Kagose Mawinda,

    So far, two have been registered with the Refugee Affairs Secretariat while documents of the remaining two leaders are still being processed.

    The Opposition presidential candidate Tundu Lissu, after losing the elections in November 2020, fled Tanzania and is currently in Belgium.

    While the former Arusha Urban Mp also moved to Kenya with his wife and three children and the family was later granted asylum in Canada.

    Magufuli was nicknamed “bulldozer” owing to his fight against corruption when he took office in 2015.

    however, the presidents have been accused of violating human rights and media freedom.

    Journalists and political and civil rights activists perceived to be anti-establishment have been thrown in prison.

    On Monday, March 15, Police stepped up arrests of people accused of spreading rumours about the health of Magufuli on social media platforms.

    On the same day, Vice president Samia Suluhu Hassan said it was normal for anybody to fall sick hence people should not exaggerate it.

    However, Prime Minister Kassim Majaliwa maintained that the president was in good health and was just overwhelmed by work.

    The president has not been seen in public since February 27.

    While this is happening, two more people are being held by police in Kilimanjaro on suspicion of spreading rumours about Magufulis’ heath bringing the total number of people arrested on the related case to four.

    Kilimanjaro Regional Police Commander Ronald Makona said the suspects were arrested on Sunday, March 13, and Saturday, March 14, respectively.

    “The Kilimanjaro Regional Police Force has arrested Peter Pius Silayo and Melchiory Prosper Shayo for distributing fabricated information on social media that senior government officials are ill, thereby violating the Cybercrimes Act,” said Makona.
