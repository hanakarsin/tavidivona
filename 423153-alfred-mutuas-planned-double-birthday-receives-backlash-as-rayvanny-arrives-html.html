    A week since news about Alfred Mutua's break up with Lillian Nganga spread like a bushfire, the Machakos governor has something to pacify his heart; his birthday.

    On August 15, Lillian announced on social media that she was no longer the First Lady, although she did not divulge the reasons behind the separation.

    Birthday in the Offing

    As it happens, the presidential aspirant shares a birthday with Tanzanian singer Raymond Shaban Mwakyusa, known affably as Rayvanny.

    The legislator and crooner were both born on August 22, Mutua in 1970 and Rayvanny in 1993, which essentially means that the good governor will be turning 51, while the singer clocks 28.

    It, therefore, felt prudent for them to hold a joint birthday party in which the singer would entertain guests with some of his hit songs among them Te Amo, Mbeleko, and Kwetu.

    Rayvanny Arrives

    Mutua announced on his social media that the guest from Tanzania touched down and was received ahead of the event.

    "Received Rayvanny last night ahead of our double party and concert on Sunday, August 22, 2021, at the Emara hotel, Ole Sereni," he wrote.

    He also urged members of the public to keep listening to the radio for a chance to win tickets for two for the event.

    Backlash

    The announcement, meant to excite the public, has instead received backlash on social media.

    A cross-section took a stab at his leadership priorities, questioning the legislator's decision to hold a lavish event when his constituents still struggle with basic needs.

    Gladys Mueni:

    "Welcome to Kenya where Governors hold a press conference on their birthdays than delivering what they promised their countrymen."

    Hon Mbaluka:

    "This is what you have subjected us to we people of Muthwani, especially Githunguri and Kinka where the road is pathetic and impassable."

    Others wondered why the legislator had to cross the borders to get an entertainer for his event when Kenya has several artistes, some of them struggling to make ends meet since the COVID-19 pandemic started.

    Is Easy:

    "Instead of you and the likes of Joho promoting Kenya musicians you just rush to import these overrated bongo musicians. I thought you were the champion of promoting local talent. So much for a leader."
