    Tusky's woes continue as regulator launches probe into retailer's bank accounts over KSh 1.2B debt

    - In recent past days, shelves in Tuskys supermarket have remained empty with the supplier lacking essential items after suppliers withdrew - The Competition Authority of Kenya (CAK) ordered the management to furnish it with bank statement after it emerged the retailer had not paid suppliers

    01 Jan 0001, 00:00

    - In recent past days, shelves in Tuskys supermarket have remained empty with the supplier lacking essential items after suppliers withdrew

    - The Competition Authority of Kenya (CAK) ordered the management to furnish it with bank statement after it emerged the retailer had not paid suppliers

    - The supermarket was asked to pay its suppliers between July 1 and July 16 after it indicated it owed them KSh 884.3 million

    - Through an independent investigation, CAK realised the retailer owed suppliers an additional 400.9 million

    The Competition Authority of Kenya (CAK) has launched investigations into Tuskys Mattresses Limited after suppliers raised complaints about a KSh 1.2 billion debt owed to them.

    The regulator asked the retailer's management to furnish it with the monthly bank statements by the close of business Friday, June 19.

    According to Business Daily, the regulator launched investigations after suppliers raised complaints that they had not been paid on time as per their contractual agreement.

    In the recent past weeks, the majority of shelves have remained empty with the Tuskys' branches lacking essential items after suppliers withdrew over delayed payments.

    On Monday, June 15, the watchdog ordered the family-owned retailer to settle the KSh 1.29 billion owed to supplies by between July 1 and July 16.

    The regulator threatened to take action against the executive officers should they fail to settle the debt or furnish CAK with bank statements, audited accounts, list of suppliers and their contracts.

    “Any person who fails to comply with the order of the authority commits an offence and risk a jail term of five years or a fine of up to KSh 10 million or both,” said CAK in a letter to the retailer.

    On May 15, the supermarket tod the regulator it owed suppliers KSh 884.3 million and later on June 12 revised the figure downwards saying it had paid some of its suppliers but failed to provide proof of payment.

    Through an independent investigation, CAK established the retailer had not paid an additional KSh 400.9 million owed to suppliers.

    READ ALSO: Eldoret: Family Bank on the spot as client decries disappearance of KSh 109K from account

    “With this background and our investigations, the Authority has established that Tusker Mattresses Limited is experiencing incidences of abuse of buyer power in the form of delays in payment of suppliers contrary to sections 24A(1) and 24A(5)(a) of the Act,” CAK wrote in a letter to the retailer.

    On its part, management blamed the COVID-19 pandemic for its woes.

    Tuskys becomes the first major retailer to face the scrutiny of CAK’s buyer power department which was created after Nakumatt Holdings collapsed with KSh 30 billion owed to suppliers.
