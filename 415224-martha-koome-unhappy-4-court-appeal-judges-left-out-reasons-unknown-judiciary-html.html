    Chief Justice Martha Koome has once again lambasted President Uhuru Kenyatta for selectively appointing 34 judges while leaving out six others.

    Uhuru had on Thursday, June 3, gazetted the appointment of 34 judges and magistrates but rejected Joel Ngugi, George Odunga, Weldon Korir and Aggrey Muchelule who had been promoted as Court of Appeal judges.

    On the other hand, magistrates Cheruiyot Judith Omange and Evans Makori were dropped from the High Court list; they had been promoted to the position of the High Court judge.

    Speaking at the swearing-in of Court of Appeal president Daniel Musinga, Koome reiterated Uhuru must appoint the remaining judges as recommended by the Judicial Service Commission.

    "As we welcome our seven new justices at the court of appeal, this is a bitter-sweet moment for the four colleagues not with us. I reiterate my position as chief justice and chair of JSC to state that all persons recommended by JSC must be appointed as judges," she insisted.

    Strained Judiciary

    She pointed out that the Judiciary was grappling with a backlog of cases due to the shortage of appellate judges occasioned by the delayed gazettement despite the JSC having forwarded the names in 2019.

    At the same time, the CJ urged the National Treasury to allocate more fund to the Judiciary to enable it to implement reforms agenda in the technology for service efficiency.

    "If we are to tackle the backlog of cases, adopt technology for efficiency, take justice closer to the people and increase the speed of resolution of cases, then we require funds. These are difficult times for all of us in Judiciary; Court of Appeal judges will now revert to seating in the court of appeal stations outside of Nairobi that have been inoperative since 2019, due to the shortage," she added.

    Uhuru's defiance

    Despite the growing anger from the Judiciary and law scholars, Uhuru defended his action saying as long he is the president, he would always be guided by integrity to choose that which is right over convenient for the sake of posterity.

    "Just like you today, I too took an oath, it is not open to me to turn a blind eye to reports of our state organs. As long as I serve as president, I will choose right over the convenient, I will choose the hard over the easy and I am not doing this for myself but for the people of Kenya and posterity," he stated.
