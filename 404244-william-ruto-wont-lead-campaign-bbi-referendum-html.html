    Deputy President William Ruto has clarified he has no plans to lead campaign against the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) referendum.

    Ruto said he is currently focused on empowering the hustler nation and will not waste time on a matter that does not change the lives of the common mwananchi.

    Speaking in Nandi county on Friday, February 26, the DP pledged to respect the will of the people whether or not they vote in support of the constitutional amendment in the referendum.

    "As the hustler nation, we have no problem with the BBI and all that they are doing to distribute powerful positions. We want to see how we can help Kenyans, hustlers, improve their businesses so that the country can move forward," he stated.

    Tribal leaders

    In an apparent reference to President Uhuru Kenyatta's State House meeting with pro-BBI political party leaders, Ruto sneered at the leaders saying the next government will not be formed by tribal chieftains.

    "They are holding meetings in Nairobi. They are telling us they are planning the next government. The 2022 government will not be formed by tribal leaders but the people at the grassroots," he added.

    Uhuru and the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) leader Raila Odinga held a meeting with ANC boss Musalia Mudavadi and Ford Kenya's Moses Wetang'ula at the State House to chart the way forward for BBI.

    The Thursday, February 25, meeting was also attended by Wiper Democratic Movement party leader Kalonzo Musyoka, KANU chairperson and Senator Gideon Moi as well as NARC's Charity Ngilu.

    A statement from the State House sent to the newsrooms later after the meeting confirmed the leaders agreed on the need for civic education on the content of the BBI bill.

    It was for this reason that another consultative meeting will be convened on March 9 to chart the way forward for the BBI ahead of its introduction in the parliament.

    "And to this end we shall hold a joint consultative meeting on the March 9, 2021, involving Members of Parliament and counties leadership. As our county representatives have shown, now is the moment for all Kenyans to put aside partisan divides and come together to build a better Kenya," they said in a joint statement.
