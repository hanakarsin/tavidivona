    KCPE 2019: Exam markers claim credibility of exam compromised, say they worked under immense pressur

    Barely a day after the release of the KCPE 2019 results, the exam markers have now revealed the tough conditions in which they marked the test, with the examiners now saying the credibility of the results was compromised.

    The exercise which was initially set to last for 10 days ended in five days, with the more than 5,000 teachers each marking more than 100 scripts per day in immense pressure.

    Speaking to KTN TV, one of the examiners claimed the marking exercise was gravely hurried and the pressure took a toll on the health of the teachers, consequently jeopardising their effectiveness.

    "We started the exercise on Monday, November 11, and after five days we were done. We worked long hours and accuracy was therefore thrown under the bus," the teacher alleged.

    READ ALSO: Singer Size 8, DJ Mo confirm birth of newborn in emotional video

    A report by the Nation also disclosed that the examiners each worked from 6am to 10pm and they were not allowed to use their electronic gadgets in the process except during breaks

    The working environment was also unfavourable as they were walking on eggshells and one single mistake could have them disciplined by the council.

    During the exercise, three examiners lost their lives.

    One of the teachers identified as Robert Kiua Muindi of Tusunini Primary School collapsed on the night of Tuesday, November 12.

    Muindi suddenly fell ill and was rushed to PCEA Kikuyu hospital where he was pronounced dead on arrival.

    The KCPE exams results were released on Monday, November 20, where the top pupil, Michael Andy Munyiri, scored 440 marks out of a possible 500.

    He was closely trailed by three candidates who tied with 439 marks.
