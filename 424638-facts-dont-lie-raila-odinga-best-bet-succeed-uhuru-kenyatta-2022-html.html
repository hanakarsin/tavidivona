    Opinion: Facts Dont Lie, Raila Odinga is the Best Bet to Succeed Uhuru Kenyatta in 2022

    The race to succeed President Uhuru Kenyatta is hotting up, with candidates busy positioning themselves to occupy the countrys top office. But unlike in the past, 2022 will be a defining year for voters as they will be confronted with two critical options; to make or break the country.

    20 May 2024, 00:00

    The race to succeed President Uhuru Kenyatta is hotting up, with candidates busy positioning themselves to occupy the country’s top office.

    But unlike in the past, 2022 will be a defining year for voters as they will be confronted with two critical options; to make or break the country.

    With the country still worryingly divided and with a majority of Kenyans still reeling under the weight of the economic ravages of COVID-19, Kenya will require an astute leader with an impeccable track record to turn things around.

    One such tested and trusted leader with an unwavering desire for a better Kenya is Opposition leader Raila Odinga. For the last 40 years, Raila has stood for equity, fairness and peaceful co-existence.

    Courtesy of the second-liberation hero who helped bring the dictatorial KANU regime to an end, a man who has tirelessly fought for the rights of ordinary Kenyans, Kenya is today a paragon of democracy.

    Peacemaker

    In all the four elections Raila has participated in, he has always allowed democracy and peace to prevail even when he felt unsatisfied with the outcome.

    When violence broke out in 2007 after the bungled presidential election, the ODM leader abandoned his hardline stance and shook hands with Kibaki, paving the way for a coalition government that restored peace and stability in Kenya.

    When he felt rigged out in 2013, Baba, popular referred to by his supporters, went to the Supreme Court and challenged Uhuru’s victory.

    When the Willy Mutunga-led bench threw out the petition, Raila, though he did not agree, accepted the deflating verdict and moved on.

    In 2018, he left many awestruck when he shook hands with Uhuru to end their long-standing political feud.

    The truce calmed down the chaotic scenes that had proceeded the 2017 presidential election that saw Uhuru’s victory nullified by the Supreme Court.

    Selfless

    Students of history have described him as Kenya’s father of democracy thanks to his consistency in fighting for freedom of speech and expression.

    Raila put his life on the line as he viciously fought against Daniel arap Moi’s one-party dictatorship.

    He was detained twice (from 1982 to 1988 and 1989 to 1991), making him Kenya’s longest-serving political detainee.

    He suffered this political persecution in the hands of a rogue regime but his ability to forgive to test, and he passed with flying colours.

    Despite enduring dehumanising torture at the dreaded Nyayo House torture chambers, he made peace with Moi and worked with his government without holding a grudge.

    The upcoming 2022 General Election is likely to present a tense political moment that could destabilise the country’s political and economic stability.

    As such, a unifying leader who stands for peace and tranquillity of the country would be a great option for Kenya.

    Development-oriented

    Raila has proved to be a development-oriented leader with a great passion for infrastructure. As Kenyan's Minister for Roads in Kibaki's government, Raila laid the ground for constructing and expanding major roads across the country.

    As Prime Minister in the coalition government, he was instrumental in conceiving mega projects initiated by the administration.

    Key among them was the construction of the Thika Superhighway and the Rural Electrification Programme.

    Some other key projects actualized during the current regime, among them the Standard Gauge Railway and the Nairobi Expressway, were also conceived during the Raila-Kibaki coalition government.

    The current constitution, which has been ranked among the most progressive in Africa and even globally, was promulgated in 2010, with Raila among its main proponents.

    Deputy President William Ruto, then the Higher Education Minister, spearheaded the “No” campaign against the constitution.

    As an experienced and visionary leader, Raila fought for devolution to enhance equity and fairness in the distribution of resources.

    Today, devolution is the single most celebrated aspect of the 2010 constitution.

    The former premier is undoubtedly Kenya’s most travelled, exposed and well-connected politician with unparalleled international appeal.

    His appointment as Africa’s High Representative for Infrastructure Development has elevated his status not only in Africa but across the globe.

    He has been involved in negotiating with development partners on behalf of many African countries, including the Democratic Republic of Congo.

    In June 2021, the multi-billion African Green Infrastructure Investment Bank (AfGIIB) appointed him to its board to tap from his experience as an astute planner and manager of public resources.

    Fixing corruption

    Raila is revered for his record of exposing wanton graft deals that have negatively impacted the country’s economy.

    He has consistently fought corruption and vouched for lifestyle audits of all public servants.

    It’s Raila who exposed the rot in the National Youth Service, prompting Uhuru to take action. The former premier also put the government on task to explain Eurobond expenditure.

    Furthermore, he recently exposed the multi-billion-shilling Kimwarer and Arror dam scandals and called for proper investigation.

    Never mind that Raila has never been in mega corruption scandals despite over the many years he has been at the top, pointing to his integrity.

    This, plus his unquestionable love for the country, is what Kenya needs to tame corruption, overborrowing and heavy taxation.

    When he stands to speak, the ODM leader cuts the image of a selfless national figure with a passion for transforming Kenya’s economic and social status.

    Uhuru has relished every moment he has worked with the Opposition chief since burying the hatched in 2018.

    Recently, the president admitted he had done a lot more working with Raila than he did while working with his deputy in the first term of his administration.

    During a tour in Ukambani, he hailed Raila as a visionary leader who firmly believes intangible infrastructure development, unlike others who believe in handout politics.

    “When Raila speaks you will hear him talking about infrastructure, infrastructure and infrastructure because he knows what it means to invest in infrastructure,” Uhuru said.

    Having worked with Kibaki and now with Uhuru, Raila is Kenya’s only hope for a stable, prosperous and secure country post-2022. He is trusted, tried and tested. He is resilient and accommodating.

    Raila’s track record puts him well ahead of his competitors when it comes to choosing the best candidate to succeed Uhuru in 2022.

    He offers a safe pair of hands that will safeguard the interests of ordinary Kenyans and take the country to a new level.
