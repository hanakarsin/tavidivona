    Ruto's Chief of Staff named in KSh 21 billion Arror, Kimwarer dams projects scam

    - Preliminary investigations by DCI revealed Ken Osinde was one of the directors of Sanlam General Insurance Ltd - The insurance firm was allegedly paid KSh 47 million by Italian company CMC di Ravenna as a subcontractor

    30 Jul 2024, 00:00

    - Preliminary investigations by DCI revealed Ken Osinde was one of the directors of Sanlam General Insurance Ltd

    - The insurance firm was allegedly paid KSh 47 million by Italian company CMC di Ravenna as a subcontractor

    - DCI is seeking to establish what kind of services Sanlam offered in relation to the Arror, Kimwarer projects

    - Several directors of various companies are being grilled by DCI regarding their involvements in the mega projects

    Deputy President William Ruto's aide, Ken Osinde, has been caught up in the multi-billion shilling Arror and Kimwarer dams scandal in which KSh 21 billion meant for the projects was allegedly siphoned by a few individuals.

    Osinde became a person of interest to the detectives after it was established a company he is associated with was subcontracted by controversial Italian company CMC di Ravenna which had been contracted by Kerio Valley Development Authority (KVDA) to construct the dams.

    Fresh details emerged on Wednesday, February 27, indicating Osinde is a director at Sanlam General Insurance Ltd, one of the companies whose bosses were summoned by the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) in connection with the Arror and Kimwarer dams scandal.

    According to the DCI boss George Kinoti, the insurer was paid KSh 47 million by CMC di Ravenna as a subcontractor in the controversial projects, but it was not clear what kind of services the insurance firm offered in return to justify the payment.

    “We are pursuing the insurance company to establish what they insured. If they insured the projects, then they will have to pay Kenyans for the loss incurred so far,” Kinoti said as quoted by Daily Nation.

    READ ALSO: Liquor producer Africa Spirits' bosses summoned by DCI over tax evasion allegations

    TUKO.co.ke understands the money paid to Sanlam by CMC di Ravenna was part of the KSh 21 billion that was wired to the Italian contractor by the National Treasury and which was to go towards construction of the Arror and Kimwarer dams in Elgeyo-Marakwet.

    The KSh 21 billion was part of KSh 65 billion that the Treasury had borrowed from foreign banks, against the advice of former Attorney General Githu Muigai, for the construction of the mega reservoirs.

    The dams were being built to boost production of hydro-electricity and irrigation within Elgeyo-Marakwet county with the aim of mitigating perennial challenges of drought.

    The projects, which were to commence in December 2017, were also geared towards boosting food production and increasing electricity megawatts to the national grid.

    Two years later, no work has began at the construction sites yet KSh 21 billion has been spent.
